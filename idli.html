Idli Recipe - Soft & Fluffy South Indian Delight

Last updated: 10/01/2025

Idli is a soft, fluffy, and healthy South Indian dish made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram). It is steamed instead of fried, making it light on the stomach and easy to digest. Idlis are often served with coconut chutney, sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew), or spicy podi (a dry chutney powder). This dish is packed with probiotics due to the fermentation process, which improves gut health and digestion. Idli is also gluten-free and low in calories, making it a great choice for a nutritious breakfast.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups idli rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (black gram, husked)
  • ½ teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil or ghee for greasing

Instructions

  • Rinse the idli rice and urad dal separately. Soak the rice and dal with fenugreek seeds for 6 hours.
  • Drain and grind the urad dal to a smooth paste, adding water gradually.
  • Grind the soaked rice separately to a slightly coarse batter.
  • Mix both batters, add salt, and let it ferment overnight (8-10 hours) in a warm place.
  • Grease idli plates with oil or ghee and pour the fermented batter into each mold.
  • Steam the idlis for about 10-12 minutes until fluffy and cooked through.
  • Serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or podi.
