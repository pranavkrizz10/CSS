Central Park

Central Park, an urban oasis in the heart of Manhattan, spans 843 acres and is one of the most visited parks in the world. Designed by Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux, it opened in 1858 and remains a cultural landmark.

The park features meadows, lakes, wooded areas, and famous attractions such as Bethesda Terrace, the Bow Bridge, and Strawberry Fields. It provides a sanctuary for wildlife and a retreat for New Yorkers and tourists alike.
