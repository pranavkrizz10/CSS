Ellis Island

Ellis Island, located in New York Harbor, was the gateway for millions of immigrants to the United States from 1892 to 1954. It served as the nation's busiest immigrant inspection station and is now part of the Statue of Liberty National Monument.

The island played a crucial role in shaping the cultural fabric of America, with over 12 million immigrants passing through its halls. Today, the Ellis Island Immigration Museum preserves this rich history, showcasing exhibits and records of immigrants who arrived there.
